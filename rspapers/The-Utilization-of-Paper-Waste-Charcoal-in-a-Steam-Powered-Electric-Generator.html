    The Utilization of Paper Waste Charcoal in a Steam Powered Electric Generator

    The Philippines is confronted with the ongoing issues of anritiiiTEg uate paper waste disposal, which remain unresolved in terms of sustainable solutions. This study endeavors to address these challenges by proposing the utilization of paper waste as a sustainable alternative fuel for electricity production, thereby providing a promising approach to mitigate these issues. With the use of a Steam-powered Electric Generator, the paper waste charcoal will be tested in terms of its potential to act as a potential fuel source. Two types of paper charcoal were produced in this study to determine which will serve as the best fuel to be used in generating electricity. The first type only contains paper and water while the second type was mixed with cornstarch solution. Upon testing, the results show that paper charcoal type 01 produces heat faster and longer steam duration compared to paper charcoal type 2. This indicates that it is more beneficial to utilize paper charcoal that is not mixed with cornstarch. Based on the findings, it can be deduced that paper charcoal possesses the potential to serve as an alternative fuel, given its comparable characteristics to commercial charcoal. However, the inadequacy of the steam-powered electric generator as a suitable mechanism hindered the proper testing and conclusive evidence regarding the performance of paper charcoal as a fuel source.
